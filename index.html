Fertilizer Advice

 

Weekly Fertilizer Usage Plan

Plan your fertilizer usage for optimal plant growth

Week 0 (Preparation Stage)

Objective: Prepare the soil for planting.

Apply basal fertilizer with a focus on phosphorus (P) and potassium (K) to encourage root development.

Example: DAP (Di-Ammonium Phosphate) or SSP (Single Super Phosphate).

Dosage: 50–60 kg P₂O₅/ha and 40–50 kg K₂O/ha.

Incorporate organic matter such as compost or well-decomposed manure (5–10 tons/ha).

Week 1 (After Transplanting or Emergence)

Objective: Encourage vigorous vegetative growth.

Apply Urea (Nitrogen source) for early growth.

Dosage: 30–40 kg N/ha.

Method: Broadcast uniformly or use as topdressing in flooded fields.

Week 2

Check for pest or weed pressure. Use integrated pest management if needed.

No additional fertilizer.

Week 3

Conduct the first topdressing of nitrogen.

Use Urea: 20–30 kg N/ha.

Apply when fields are slightly drained or just before irrigation.

Week 4

Apply a second dose of nitrogen if tillering is not sufficient.

Urea: 20–30 kg N/ha.

Include a micronutrient mix with zinc sulfate (5–10 kg/ha) to prevent deficiencies.

Week 5

Apply potassium sulfate (K₂SO₄) or Muriate of Potash (MOP) for panicle development.

Dosage: 40–50 kg K₂O/ha.

Method: Incorporate into soil before irrigation.

Week 6

Apply a small amount of nitrogen for flowering boost (optional if signs of deficiency are observed).

Urea: 10–15 kg N/ha.

Week 7

Spray foliar fertilizers containing boron (1–2 kg/ha) and silicon to improve grain quality and stress tolerance.

Week 8

Maintain proper water levels and avoid fertilizer application to prevent overgrowth or lodging.

Week 9–12 (Ripening Stage)

Objective: Complete grain filling and hardening.

Avoid further nitrogen to prevent delays in maturity.

Use potassium if needed to strengthen grain formation.
